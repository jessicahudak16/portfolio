Lockbox

Overview

For my Introduction to Engineering Design (ME 2900) prototype project, I transformed a shoebox into an imaginative OSU-themed lockbox, complete with an Arduino Uno, keypad, servo, shift register, and LEDs.

The prototype features a keypad on the lid that allows the user to enter a password. With Arduino software, I programmed the default password to be “43210” (the zip code of Columbus). The Arduino waits until five consecutive digits that match this designated sequence are pressed.

Once the correct password is entered, the servo rotates 90 degrees from the locked position to the unlocked position. I engraved cuts in the top flap of the lid and front face of the box that match the dimensions of the servo arm. These cuts prevent the box from being opened when the servo is in the locked position, but allow the box to be opened when the servo is in the unlocked position.

Once the servo is in the unlocked position, the shift register orders the red and white LEDs that constitute a Block-O formation to blink. Additionally, the RGB LED changes color.

After the user accesses the contents stored inside the lockbox, he or she simply presses either the asterisk key or the pound sign key on the keypad in order to signal the servo to rotate 90 degrees, back to the locked position.

You can watch a demonstration at https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OXFjG9pLCf4.
